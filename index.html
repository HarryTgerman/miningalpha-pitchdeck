The core data in a multi-trillion dollar asset class is locked up

  • Mining valuation runs on supply data: ounces in the ground, grade, production cost, AISC. Every serious investor needs this.
  • It lives in 200-page PDFs. Every junior publishes NI 43-101 or JORC technical reports. Thousands of them. No structured database below the majors.
  • The structured version exists, but it is gated behind terminals built for institutions:
    • S&P Capital IQ Pro (Metals & Mining): used by mining funds and M&A desks. $15k to $30k per seat per year.
    • Wood Mackenzie: used by majors, commodity traders, sovereign wealth. Five to six figure annual subscriptions. Single reports $6,750 each.
    • Benchmark Mineral Intelligence: authority on lithium, cobalt, rare earths. Prices settle ICE futures. Enterprise only, quote-based.
    • Bloomberg Terminal: $25k per seat, mining coverage is shallow.
  • Result: everyone below institutional flies blind. Retail has nothing. Mid-tier funds screenshot PDFs. AI agents cannot ingest mining data at all.
